About Me

Hello! I am Dr. Ryan M. Dreifuerst. I recently successfully defended my PhD at North Carolina State University on March 20th. This marked the culmination of five years of research working alongside Prof. Robert W. Heath Jr. across both the University of Texas at Austin and NC State.

My core focus lies at the intersection of signal processing and machine learning for next-generation wireless communication networks. I am passionate about solving hardware and physical layer challenges in 5G and 6G systems.

I am thrilled to announce that I am beginning my post-graduate professional journey at MITRE.

Research Focus

Massive MIMO & Beam Management

Innovating beamforming and codebook architectures to enable larger array sizes and handle extreme MIMO scenarios in sub-6 GHz and mmWave environments.

Machine Learning for Wireless

Leveraging deep learning, reinforcement learning, and neural networks to optimize coverage, capacity, resource scheduling, and carrier frequency offset estimation.

Next-Gen Signal Processing

Developing low-resolution sinusoid decomposition and advanced CSI (Channel State Information) Type-II feedback mechanisms to reduce overhead and improve network efficiency.
