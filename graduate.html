Deep Learning-based Carrier Frequency Offset Estimation with 1-Bit ADCs

Ryan M. Dreifuerst, Robert W. Heath Jr, Mandar Kulknari

Low resolution architectures are a power efficient solution for high bandwidth communication at millimeter wave and TeraHertz frequencies. In such systems, carrier synchronization is important yet has not received much attention in prior work. In this paper, we develop and analyze deep learning architectures that are able to accurately predict carrier frequency offset from 1-bit quantized data with less pilot data and lower signal to noise ratios than traditional signal processing methods. We compare each architecture style in terms of scalability for future 5G and 6G base stations. We demonstrate different training methods and evaluate how the models perform when trained in each method. Finally, simulation results of the models' computational complexity, scalability, and mean squared error versus classic signal processing techniques show the effectiveness of deep learning for low resolution frequency estimation.
